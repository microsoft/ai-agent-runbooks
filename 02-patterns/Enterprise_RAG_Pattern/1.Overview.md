# Enterprise RAG Pattern Overview

The Enterprise RAG (Red, Amber, Green) Pattern provides a structured approach to evaluate and categorize projects, initiatives, or elements within an organization. This methodology uses a color-coded system to quickly communicate status and priorities, enabling efficient decision-making and resource allocation.

## Why Use the Enterprise RAG Pattern?
- **Clarity**: It simplifies complex data by categorizing it into easily understandable segments.
- **Quick Decision Making**: Helps stakeholders quickly assess where attention is needed most, streamlining discussions and actions.
- **Resource Allocation**: Allows for optimal resource allocation by identifying which areas require immediate focus versus those that are on track.

## When to Use the Enterprise RAG Pattern?
- At project kickoff to establish baseline statuses.
- During regular project reviews to update stakeholders on progress.
- In risk management sessions to identify and prioritize potential threats or delays.

## Quick Navigation
- **Red**: Immediate attention needed. Significant issues or risks that jeopardize success must be addressed.
- **Amber**: Caution warranted. There are some risks or delays that require monitoring but are manageable with additional oversight.
- **Green**: All systems go. The project is on track and no significant issues are present.
